# CSR Compliance (India)

## When to Use

Load this skill when the conversation involves:
- Corporate Social Responsibility obligations under Indian law
- MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs) compliance and reporting
- CSR spend calculation, budgeting, or tracking
- Schedule VII eligible activities
- CSR Committee formation or responsibilities
- CSR-1 registration for implementing agencies
- Annual Action Plan preparation
- Board Report CSR disclosures
- Unspent CSR amounts and transfer to funds
- Impact assessment requirements
- Any of the 11 MCA CSR report formats
- Penalties or consequences for CSR non-compliance

## Domain Knowledge

### Legal Foundation

**Companies Act, 2013 — Section 135** is the primary legislation governing CSR in India. It applies to companies meeting any of the following thresholds in the immediately preceding financial year:

| Threshold | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Net worth | >= INR 500 crore |
| Turnover | >= INR 1,000 crore |
| Net profit | >= INR 5 crore |

Once a company qualifies, it must:
1. Constitute a CSR Committee of the Board
2. Formulate a CSR Policy
3. Spend at least 2% of average net profits of the three immediately preceding financial years on CSR activities
4. Report CSR activities in the Board Report

**CSR Rules, 2014** (Companies (Corporate Social Responsibility Policy) Rules, 2014) provide the operational framework. These were significantly amended in 2021 via the Companies (CSR Policy) Amendment Rules, 2021, effective 22 January 2021.

### CSR Committee

**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 directors, at least 1 independent director
- For private companies / companies not required to have an independent director: 2 or more directors
- For foreign companies: at least 2 persons, one being a person resident in India as per Section 2(78) of the Companies Act

**Responsibilities:**
- Formulate and recommend CSR Policy to the Board
- Recommend the amount of CSR expenditure
- Monitor the CSR Policy implementation
- Prepare and recommend Annual Action Plan

**Dissolution:** If the company ceases to meet the threshold criteria for three consecutive financial years, the CSR Committee can be dissolved. However, CSR obligations for the years when thresholds were met must still be fulfilled.

### CSR Spend Calculation

**Formula:** 2% of average net profits of three immediately preceding financial years.

**Net profit calculation (Section 198):**
- Computed as per the profit and loss account prepared under Section 198
- Excludes profits from overseas branches/divisions
- Excludes dividend received from other Indian companies meeting Section 135 criteria
- Includes profits from sale of undertaking or property (after deducting book value and expenses)

**Important nuances:**
- A company in its first or second year of existence uses available years (1 or 2) for the average
- If the company has no average net profit or negative average net profit, no CSR obligation arises (but voluntary CSR is permitted)
- Profits from overseas branches are excluded for the purpose of computing CSR obligation

### Schedule VII — Eligible CSR Activities

Schedule VII lists broad categories of eligible activities. Key areas:

1. **Eradicating hunger, poverty, and malnutrition** — promoting health care including preventive health, sanitation, safe drinking water. Contribution to Swachh Bharat Kosh
2. **Promoting education** — including special education and employment-enhancing vocation skills, livelihood enhancement projects
3. **Gender equality and women empowerment** — setting up homes, hostels for women and orphans; old age homes, day care centres
4. **Environmental sustainability** — ecological balance, conservation of natural resources, animal welfare, agroforestry, maintaining quality of soil, air, water
5. **Protection of national heritage, art, and culture** — restoration of buildings, sites of historical importance, public libraries, traditional arts and handicrafts
6. **Measures for the benefit of armed forces veterans, war widows, and their dependents** — including contribution to Central Armed Forces Veterans Welfare Fund
7. **Training to promote rural, nationally recognized, Paralympic, or Olympic sports**
8. **Contribution to PM's National Relief Fund** or any other Central Government fund for socio-economic development, relief, and welfare
9. **Contribution to technology incubators** (within academic institutions approved by the Central Government)
10. **Rural development projects**
11. **Slum area development** (Explanation: 'slum area' as declared by government or State legislature)
12. **Disaster management** including relief, rehabilitation, and reconstruction
13. **Contribution to State Disaster Management Authority** (not permitted to count towards CSR from FY 2023-24 as per certain clarifications; verify latest position)

**What does NOT qualify:**
- Activities undertaken outside India (except training of Indian sports teams for international events)
- Activities that benefit only employees and their families
- One-off events like marathons, awards, sponsorships of TV programs (unless directly tied to Schedule VII)
- Political donations
- Activities undertaken in the normal course of business

### Annual Action Plan

Post the 2021 amendment, the CSR Committee must formulate and recommend an **Annual Action Plan** to the Board, containing:
- List of CSR projects/programs proposed
- Manner of execution (direct or through implementing agency)
- Modalities of utilization of funds
- Implementation schedule
- Monitoring and reporting mechanism
- Details of need and impact assessment (if applicable)

The Board may alter the Annual Action Plan at any time during the financial year based on recommendation of the CSR Committee.

### Ongoing Projects vs New Projects

**Ongoing project:** A multi-year project (up to 3 years, excluding the year of commencement; extendable by up to 1 year with Board approval) that was approved by the Board in the Annual Action Plan of a previous year and for which unspent amounts are transferred to a special Unspent CSR Account.

**New project:** Any project commenced and completed within the same financial year, or a new multi-year project commencing in the current financial year.

This distinction matters for the treatment of **unspent CSR amounts**.

### Unspent CSR Amount

**For ongoing projects:**
- Unspent amount must be transferred to a special bank account ("Unspent Corporate Social Responsibility Account") within 30 days of the end of the financial year
- Must be spent within 3 financial years from the date of transfer
- If not spent within 3 years, transferred to a Schedule VII fund (PM CARES Fund, PM National Relief Fund, etc.) within 30 days

**For amounts not related to ongoing projects:**
- Any unspent amount (from the obligatory 2%) that is not attributable to an ongoing project must be transferred to a Schedule VII fund within 6 months of the end of the financial year

### Impact Assessment

**Mandatory when:** A CSR project has an outlay of INR 1 crore or more AND has been completed at least one year before the assessment.

**Requirements:**
- Must be conducted by an independent agency
- The agency should have expertise in social impact assessment
- Assessment report must be annexed to the Annual Report on CSR
- Cost of impact assessment can be counted as CSR expenditure (but within the 5% admin cap)

**Assessment components:** The MCA has not prescribed a rigid format, but best practice includes:
- Project objectives vs outcomes achieved
- Beneficiary reach and demographics
- Sustainability analysis
- Lessons learned and recommendations

### CSR-1 Registration

Post the 2021 amendment, every entity (other than the company itself) that intends to undertake CSR activities as an implementing agency must obtain a **CSR-1 registration** from the MCA.

**Eligible implementing agencies:**
- A company established under Section 8 of the Companies Act (or Section 25 of the 1956 Act)
- A registered public trust
- A registered society under Societies Registration Act, 1860
- An entity established under an Act of Parliament or State legislature

**CSR-1 process:**
- File Form CSR-1 on the MCA portal
- Provide PAN, registration details, and CSR activity details
- A unique CSR Registration Number is issued
- This number is required for the donor company's CSR reporting

### Board Report Disclosures

The Board Report must include an annual report on CSR in the format prescribed in the Annexure to the CSR Rules. Key elements:

1. Brief outline of the CSR Policy and overview of projects
2. Composition of the CSR Committee
3. Average net profit for the last three financial years
4. Prescribed CSR expenditure (2% of average net profit)
5. Details of CSR expenditure during the financial year
6. Amount unspent and reason
7. Details of ongoing projects
8. Details of excess CSR spent (if any; can be set off against up to 3 succeeding financial years, subject to Board resolution)
9. Impact assessment details (if applicable)
10. CFO or person responsible for financial management certifying that funds were utilized for the purpose and manner approved by the Board
11. Responsibility statement by the CSR Committee

### The 11 MCA Reports and Forms

| # | Report/Form | Purpose | Filed With | Frequency |
|---|-------------|---------|------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Annual Report on CSR (Annexure I)** | Detailed report on CSR activities annexed to Board Report | MCA (as part of Annual Report) | Annual |
| 2 | **Annual Report on CSR (Annexure II)** | Format for companies with average CSR obligation <= INR 10 lakh | MCA (as part of Annual Report) | Annual |
| 3 | **Form CSR-1** | Registration of implementing agencies | MCA portal | One-time |
| 4 | **Form CSR-2** | Report on CSR by every company to which Section 135 applies | MCA portal | Annual (filed with AOC-4) |
| 5 | **Form AOC-4** | Financial statements and Board Report (contains CSR annexure) | MCA (ROC) | Annual |
| 6 | **Annual Action Plan** | Internal document; CSR projects/programs planned for the year | Board approval (not filed with MCA) | Annual |
| 7 | **Impact Assessment Report** | Independent assessment of projects >= INR 1 crore | Annexed to Annual Report on CSR | As applicable |
| 8 | **Board Resolution (CSR Policy)** | Approval of CSR Policy and Annual Action Plan | Internal; maintained in board minutes | As needed |
| 9 | **CFO Certification** | Certification that CSR funds were utilized as approved | Part of Annual Report on CSR | Annual |
| 10 | **Unspent CSR Account Statement** | Tracking of amounts in Unspent CSR Account | Internal/statutory audit | Annual |
| 11 | **Set-off Declaration** | Board resolution for setting off excess CSR spend | Internal; disclosed in Board Report | As applicable |

**Note:** The exact list of "11 MCA reports" can vary by interpretation. The above covers the primary compliance documents. Some practitioners also count the separate disclosures in the Directors' Responsibility Statement and the Corporate Governance Report (for listed companies) as distinct reporting obligations.

### Form CSR-2

- Introduced in 2021; mandatory for all companies to which Section 135 applies
- Filed as an addendum to Form AOC-4
- Contains project-wise details of CSR spend
- Must be filed electronically on the MCA21 portal
- Due date aligns with AOC-4 filing deadline

### Excess CSR Spend and Set-Off

If a company spends more than its 2% obligation in a financial year:
- The excess can be set off against the CSR obligation in the following 3 financial years
- Requires a Board resolution
- Must be disclosed in the Annual Report on CSR
- The set-off is available only if the excess was not treated as CSR expenditure at the behest of the company's Board in any previous financial year

### Administrative Overhead Cap

- Maximum **5%** of total CSR expenditure for the financial year can be spent on administrative overheads
- Administrative overheads include salaries of CSR team, travel expenses for monitoring, office expenses directly attributable to CSR
- Does not include program delivery costs (those are direct CSR spend)

### Capital Assets

CSR expenditure **shall not** be used to acquire capital assets that are owned by the company or its employees. However:
- Capital assets created through CSR must be held by:
  - A Section 8 company, or
  - A registered public trust or society, or
  - The beneficiaries of the CSR project, or
  - A public authority
- This is a key compliance area — many companies historically created assets (buildings, vehicles) under CSR that they then used for their own operations

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

**Under Companies Act, 2013 (post 2020 amendment):**

If a company fails to comply with Section 135(5) (spending obligation) and Section 135(6) (transfer of unspent amount):

| Violation | Company Penalty | Officer Penalty |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Default in spending | Up to INR 1 crore or twice the unspent amount, whichever is less | Imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine up to INR 5 lakh, or both |
| Default in transfer to Unspent CSR Account | Same as above | Same as above |
| Default in transfer to Schedule VII fund | Same as above | Same as above |

**Note:** The 2020 amendment (effective 22 January 2021) decriminalized certain CSR offences. Non-spending is now treated as a civil offence (monetary penalty) rather than a criminal one, except in cases of fraud. However, repeated default or failure to transfer unspent amounts can still attract criminal provisions.

### Tax Treatment of CSR Expenditure

- CSR expenditure is **not deductible** as a business expense under Section 37 of the Income Tax Act, 1961
- However, if the CSR activity also qualifies under a specific section (e.g., Section 80G for donations), the deduction may be available under that specific section — but this is an area of frequent litigation and CBDT circulars
- Companies should obtain a tax opinion for borderline cases

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Section 135** | Primary CSR provision in the Companies Act, 2013 |
| **Schedule VII** | List of eligible CSR activities under the Companies Act |
| **CSR Committee** | Board-level committee responsible for CSR policy and oversight |
| **CSR-1** | MCA registration form for implementing agencies |
| **CSR-2** | Annual e-form filed with MCA reporting CSR project details |
| **Annual Action Plan** | Year-wise plan of CSR projects approved by the Board |
| **Ongoing Project** | Multi-year CSR project (up to 3+1 years) |
| **Unspent CSR Account** | Dedicated bank account for unspent CSR amounts related to ongoing projects |
| **Impact Assessment** | Independent evaluation required for projects >= INR 1 crore |
| **CFO Certification** | Mandatory sign-off that CSR funds were used as approved |
| **Average Net Profit** | 3-year rolling average of net profit under Section 198, used to calculate 2% obligation |
| **Set-off** | Carrying forward excess CSR spend to offset future obligations (up to 3 years) |
| **Admin Overhead** | CSR administrative costs, capped at 5% of total CSR expenditure |
| **Implementing Agency** | Entity (Section 8 company, trust, society) registered under CSR-1 to execute CSR projects |
| **AOC-4** | Annual MCA filing for financial statements, to which CSR-2 is attached |

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Confusing turnover threshold with net profit threshold.** A company can trigger CSR obligations through any one of the three thresholds (net worth, turnover, net profit) — it does not need to meet all three.

2. **Forgetting the 3-year average.** The 2% is on the average of three preceding financial years, not the current year's profit. New companies use available years.

3. **Treating all NGO donations as CSR.** The implementing agency must have CSR-1 registration (post-2021), and the activity must fall under Schedule VII.

4. **Capital asset trap.** Creating capital assets that remain with the company is a violation. Assets must vest with specified entities or beneficiaries.

5. **Ignoring the Unspent CSR Account timeline.** Unspent amounts for ongoing projects must be transferred within 30 days of FY end; unspent amounts for non-ongoing projects must go to a Schedule VII fund within 6 months. Missing these deadlines triggers penalties.

6. **Impact assessment scope creep.** The INR 1 crore threshold is per-project outlay, not per-year. A project spending INR 40 lakh/year for 3 years (total INR 1.2 crore) triggers the requirement.

7. **Admin overhead misclassification.** Program delivery costs (training materials, field staff salaries for project execution) are direct CSR costs, not admin overhead. Only CSR management/monitoring costs count toward the 5% cap.

8. **Assuming CSR spend is tax-deductible.** It is not deductible under Section 37. Specific deductions under other sections (like 80G) may apply but require careful analysis.

9. **Not updating the Annual Action Plan.** Changes to CSR projects mid-year require CSR Committee recommendation and Board approval to amend the Annual Action Plan.

10. **Treating CSR as philanthropy.** Post-2021, CSR is a regulated compliance obligation with specific documentation, reporting, and transfer requirements — not discretionary charity.
